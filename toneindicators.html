TONE TAGS

What is tone?
Tone is what a phrase or statement means or comes across as despite what it's actually saying. Examples of tone could be serious, joking, lighthearted, etc.
What are tone tags?
Tone tags aka tone indicators are things you can include with text to indicate what the tone of it is. Always starts with a slash.
Why are they used?
Like me, some people have difficulty picking up on tone. Communicating through text only makes this harder due to lack of audio and physical clues. This includes voice inflection, body language, facial expressions, etc. Tagging what tone you are using can be very helpful for others understanding of what you're saying, clarification, avoiding miscommunications, etc.
When/Where should they be used?
They can be used any time or place you may be communicating through text.
Keep in mind...
Do not mock the use of tone tags. You may not need them, but some people do. Not everyone can tell when something is a joke or if you're mad. Be respectful! So, using a tone tag isn't an excuse to be mean. You can't say something not cool and then put /j after it to get away with it.

Try to put tone tag(s) in your original post. If you comment it underneath your original post not everyone will see it. Don't misuse tone tags "as a joke" to confuse others. Tone tags are meant to clarify.

You do not have to use all these tone tags! My site's goal is to be the biggest site of helpful resources, not only for you but for me too. You may disagree on using tone tags but keep in mind that those who don't understand tone well with texts, please be patient with them. Including myself.

I rarely use tone tags, but personally I love them when I do use them. Sometimes others use multiple, sometimes only one, sometimes none at all. If someone asks for clarification just explain your mood to them.
